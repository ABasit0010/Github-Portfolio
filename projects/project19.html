Custom PCB Layout for Tweenbot

Abstract

The Tweenbot PCB was designed to provide a compact, efficient, and reliable control board for a small autonomous robot platform. It integrates the microcontroller, motor driver, and power management circuits into a single board, reducing wiring complexity and improving mechanical stability.

Objectives

  • Design a custom PCB to house all control and power components of the Tweenbot.
  • Provide direct connection points for motors, sensors, and battery.
  • Optimize size for integration into small robotic chassis.

Hardware Components

  • ATmega328P Microcontroller
  • L298N Motor Driver IC
  • Voltage Regulators (5 V and 3.3 V)
  • Pin Headers for Ultrasonic Sensor, IR Sensors
  • Battery Connector (Li-ion 7.4 V)
  • Decoupling Capacitors & Flyback Diodes
  • FR4 PCB (Double Layer, 1.6 mm)

System Design & Functionality

The ATmega328P handles robot navigation and control logic, interfacing with ultrasonic and IR sensors for obstacle detection. The L298N driver controls two DC motors, enabling forward, reverse, and turning motions. The board includes power regulation to ensure stable operation for logic (5 V) and motor power (up to 12 V).

Technical Specifications

  • Operating Voltage: 7–12 V DC
  • Logic Voltage: 5 V, Motor Voltage: up to 12 V
  • Motor Channels: 2 (bidirectional)
  • Sensor Inputs: 3 digital, 2 analog
  • PCB Size: 70 mm × 60 mm

PCB Design Process

The PCB was designed in KiCad with attention to proper motor current trace widths and separation of high-current and logic grounds. Flyback diodes were placed near the L298N outputs to protect against voltage spikes. Mounting holes were added for chassis integration.

Key Circuit Snippet

// Motor control example
digitalWrite(MOTOR_LEFT_FORWARD, HIGH);
digitalWrite(MOTOR_LEFT_BACKWARD, LOW);
digitalWrite(MOTOR_RIGHT_FORWARD, HIGH);
digitalWrite(MOTOR_RIGHT_BACKWARD, LOW);

Testing & Results

The PCB was assembled and tested with the Tweenbot chassis. All motors responded correctly to control signals, and sensor readings were stable. The board demonstrated reliable operation with minimal noise interference.

Conclusion

The custom PCB for Tweenbot significantly reduced wiring complexity and improved performance stability. Its modular design allows for future expansions, such as wireless control modules or additional sensors.
