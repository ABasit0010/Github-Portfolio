Three-Channel Power Supply

This project involved designing and implementing a three-channel regulated DC power supply that converts AC mains voltage into three separate outputs: a variable 1.5 V to 24 V channel, a fixed –12 V channel, and a fixed +5 V channel. Each channel was designed with different regulation techniques, providing outputs with current ratings of 1 A, 0.5 A, and 0.5 A respectively.

Specifications

  • Variable Output: 1.5 V – 24 V DC @ 1 A
  • Fixed Output 1: –12 V DC @ 0.5 A
  • Fixed Output 2: +5 V DC @ 0.5 A
  • AC Input: 230 V mains

Variable 1.5 V – 24 V Power Supply

  • Transformer: 26 V secondary
  • Bridge Rectifier: Converts AC to pulsating DC
  • Filter Capacitors: 4700 µF (C3) and 100 µF (C4) to smooth output
  • Voltage Regulator: LM317 adjustable regulator
  • Control: Potentiometer (4.7 kΩ) and resistors to set output voltage
  • Output: 1.3 V (min) to 24.1 V (max)

The LM317 was configured for adjustable output using the formula: Vout = 1.25 × (1 + R2 / R1) Capacitor sizing was calculated using C = I / (2 × f × Vripple).

Fixed –12 V Power Supply

  • Transformer: 12.1 V secondary
  • Bridge Rectifier + Filter Capacitor: 1000 µF
  • Zener Diode: 12 V in reverse bias to generate negative voltage reference
  • Pass Transistor: TIP42 for current amplification
  • Output: –12 V regulated at 0.5 A

Fixed +5 V Power Supply

  • Transformer: 13.1 V secondary
  • Bridge Rectifier + Filter Capacitor: 2200 µF
  • Zener Diodes: Dual 5.6 V zeners for stable reference
  • Pass Transistors: TIP41 pair for current handling
  • Output: 4.8 V measured (close to theoretical 4.3 V)

Overall Simulations

The full system was simulated to validate regulation performance and ripple suppression. All three outputs operated within target specifications under load.

Conclusion

The three-channel power supply met all design objectives, producing regulated outputs of 1.3 V–24.1 V (variable), –12 V, and +5 V. Component choices such as LM317, zener diodes, and pass transistors ensured stability and adequate current supply. Ripple was minimized with appropriate capacitor sizing. The design is well-suited for lab bench applications requiring multiple DC voltage levels.
