Torch Light Custom PCB Layout Design

Abstract

This project involves designing a custom PCB for a compact, portable torch light powered by rechargeable batteries. The PCB integrates LED lighting, current limiting, charging circuitry, and an on/off control switch in a minimal footprint suitable for handheld devices.

Objectives

  • Design a PCB for efficient LED torch operation.
  • Integrate battery charging circuit on the PCB.
  • Ensure compact and ergonomic design for handheld use.

Hardware Components

  • High-Brightness White LEDs (SMD type)
  • Rechargeable Li-ion Battery (18650 cell)
  • TP4056 Li-ion Charging Module IC
  • Current Limiting Resistors
  • Slide Switch / Push Button Switch
  • PCB substrate: FR4, 1.6 mm thickness

System Design & Functionality

The torch PCB is designed with LED drivers positioned to maximize light output. The TP4056 chip handles battery charging via micro-USB. The LEDs are connected through current-limiting resistors to ensure safe operation. The PCB layout was optimized to reduce track resistance and heat build-up.

Technical Specifications

  • LED Forward Voltage: 3.0–3.2 V
  • LED Current: 20 mA each
  • Battery Capacity: 2200 mAh
  • Charging Current: 1 A
  • Board Dimensions: 45 mm × 20 mm

PCB Design Process

The design was completed in EasyEDA with precise placement of LEDs in series-parallel configuration. Ground and VCC planes were used to improve current flow. The charging circuit was isolated from the LED path with proper trace separation to avoid noise coupling.

Key Circuit Snippet

LED+  ----[Rlimit]----|>|---- GND
Battery+ --- TP4056 --- USB 5V

Testing & Results

The torch provided continuous illumination for over 4 hours on a single charge. The charging circuit worked as expected, with LED brightness remaining constant until low battery cutoff.

Conclusion

The Torch Light Custom PCB design successfully integrates LED lighting and charging functionality in a small form factor. Future upgrades may include adjustable brightness levels and waterproof casing.
