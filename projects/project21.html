Custom PCB Layout for Breadboard Power Supply

Abstract

This project features the design of a compact, dual-voltage breadboard power supply PCB that directly plugs into a standard breadboard. It provides regulated 5 V and 3.3 V outputs from a common DC input, making it highly suitable for prototyping microcontroller and sensor-based circuits.

Objectives

  • Design a compact PCB that fits directly onto a breadboard.
  • Provide selectable regulated voltages (5 V and 3.3 V).
  • Include overcurrent protection and an onboard power indicator.

Hardware Components

  • AMS1117-5.0 Voltage Regulator
  • AMS1117-3.3 Voltage Regulator
  • Electrolytic Capacitors for input/output smoothing
  • Ceramic Capacitors for high-frequency noise filtering
  • DC Barrel Jack (5.5 mm)
  • 2× Male Pin Headers (for breadboard power rails)
  • LED Power Indicator with current-limiting resistor
  • FR4 PCB, 1.6 mm thickness

System Design & Functionality

The PCB takes in a DC input voltage (7 V–12 V) through the barrel jack or optional USB input. It uses two AMS1117 linear regulators to supply 5 V and 3.3 V rails, selectable via jumper pins for each breadboard rail. Capacitors at the regulator input and output ensure stable voltage delivery with minimal ripple. An onboard LED indicates power status.

Technical Specifications

  • Input Voltage: 7 V – 12 V DC
  • Output Voltage: Selectable 5 V or 3.3 V for each rail
  • Max Output Current: 800 mA (per regulator)
  • PCB Size: 50 mm × 25 mm
  • Mounting: Direct breadboard insertion

PCB Design Process

Designed in EasyEDA, the layout minimizes track length between the regulator, capacitors, and output pins to reduce noise and improve stability. Thick copper pours were used for ground and Vcc to handle higher currents. The design includes silkscreen labels for easy jumper configuration and connection identification.

Key Circuit Snippet

// Voltage Regulation using AMS1117:
Vout = 1.25 * (1 + R2 / R1) + Iadj * R2
// For fixed versions, internal resistors set the output to 3.3V or 5V.

Testing & Results

The breadboard power supply delivered stable 5 V and 3.3 V outputs under load, with less than 30 mV ripple. It fit securely on the breadboard and powered ESP32, Arduino, and sensor modules without voltage drops. The onboard LED provided clear power indication.

Conclusion

This custom PCB provides a reliable, easy-to-use power source for breadboard prototyping. Its compact design, dual voltage outputs, and ease of use make it a must-have tool for electronics hobbyists and engineers.
